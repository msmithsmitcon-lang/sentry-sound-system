## CORE BUSINESS SERVICES
*These are why customers subscribe to Sentry Sound.*

### 1. Music Protection Service
What it does: Song registration, evidence capture, SHA-256 proof of
creation, collaboration certificates, ownership documentation.
Why it matters: An artist's music is officially documented and
protected from the moment it is created.

### 2. Collaboration Service
What it does: Contributor management, split agreements, digital
confirmation, team and collaborator workflows.
Why it matters: Every person who contributed to a song is recorded
and every share is documented before the song leaves the studio.

### 3. Compliance & Registration Service
What it does: CMO readiness evaluation, SAMRO/CAPASSO/SAMPRA
Submission Ready Pack generation, submission lifecycle tracking.
Why it matters: Artists can submit correctly to collecting societies
without needing to understand the administrative complexity.

### 4. Royalty Intelligence Service
What it does: Royalty statement ingestion, income tracking, payout
readiness, reconciliation, per-artist roster breakdown.
Why it matters: Artists and labels know exactly what they are owed,
from which source, and when.

### 5. Release Management Service
What it does: Distribution preparation, release planning, catalogue
management, release lifecycle tracking.
Why it matters: Music moves from creation to release with a clear,
managed process and a complete operational record.

### 6. Business Operations Service
What it does: Finance, contracts, CRM, workspace administration,
reporting, organisational management.
Why it matters: An independent artist or boutique label can run
their music business with the same operational discipline as a
major — without a team of administrators.

## VALUE-ADDED SERVICES
*Specialist services that extend the core platform.
Available as upgrades or ecosystem add-ons.
An artist can use Sentry Sound without these.*

### 7. Audio Production Service
Delivered by: Chronicle DAWG
What it does: Automated mastering, audio readiness assessment,
technical audio preparation, genre-guided processing chains,
export packaging for platform ingestion.
Why it matters: A producer can upload a track and receive a
technically prepared, industry-ready master without specialist
audio engineering knowledge.
Note: A standard Sentry Sound subscriber may never use this.
A producer may subscribe primarily because of it.

### 8. Managed Compliance Service
Status: Future — not yet built
What it does: Assisted CMO submissions, managed registration,
evidence verification, compliance audits, managed renewals,
regulator liaison, publishing administration, enterprise compliance.
Why it matters: For artists or labels who need guided assistance
rather than self-service compliance tools.
Note: Named "Managed Compliance" not "Professional Submissions"
to allow this service to evolve across the full compliance
lifecycle without being tied to one workflow.

## ENABLEMENT SERVICES
*Makes the platform usable. Included in every tier.
Not a standalone value proposition — a foundation for all others.*

### 9. Platform Guidance
What it does: Contextual help, task-level explanations, onboarding
flow, in-platform guidance at the moment of need.
Why it matters: Artists do not need to become experts in music
business administration to use Sentry Sound correctly. The platform
explains only what is relevant to the task at hand.
Note: This replaces the "Academy" sidebar item. The correct label
is "Help & Guides" or "Platform Guidance" — never "Academy."

### 10. Academy Pathway
What it does: A signpost inside Sentry Sound pointing toward the
Sentry Sound Academy institution. Shows artists that their platform
activity builds toward a nationally recognised qualification.
Why it matters: Creates the connection between the operational
platform and the separate accreditation institution without
conflating the two products.
Note: The Academy Pathway is NOT the Academy. The standalone
Sentry Sound Academy is a separate institution with its own
curriculum, qualifications, and enrolment. The pathway inside
Sentry Sound is a doorway only.

## GOVERNING RULES

### Rule 1 — The Feature Test
Every future feature, screen, or capability must answer:
"Which Business Service does this belong to?"
If the answer is "None" — it is either a new Business Service
or it does not belong in Sentry Sound.

### Rule 2 — The Subscription Rule
Subscriptions are bundles of Core Business Services
at different depths and scales.
Value-Added Services are upsells or separate line items.
Enablement Services are included in every tier.

### Rule 3 — The Academy Separation Rule
The Sentry Sound Academy is never a Sentry Sound feature.
It is a separate institution.
Sentry Sound contains only the Academy Pathway — a doorway.
The Academy institution has its own product, brand, and enrolment.

### Rule 4 — The Language Rule
Navigation reflects Business Services — not system modules.
Marketing communicates customer value — not internal architecture.
Features exist only to deliver Business Services.
The three languages (Engineering / Product / Customer) are kept
separate and translated deliberately — never conflated.
