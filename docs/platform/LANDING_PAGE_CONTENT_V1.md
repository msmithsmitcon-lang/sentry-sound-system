# Sentry Sound Landing Page Content V1

## 1. Purpose

This document is the source of truth for Sentry Sound landing-page messaging.

The landing page should introduce Sentry Sound as a modern SaaS workspace for music operations, rights readiness, contributor coordination, evidence visibility, workflow tracking, and future operational expansion.

The page must stay practical, grounded, and easy to understand. It should not overclaim production integrations, royalty automation, AI automation, billing, or regulator submission capabilities that are not active yet.

## 2. Core Positioning

Primary positioning:

The platform that keeps music operations organized.

Supporting idea:

Sentry Sound helps music teams understand what is complete, what is missing, what is blocked, what needs attention, and what can safely happen next.

## 3. Hero Messaging

Recommended hero headline:

The platform that keeps music operations organized.

Recommended hero subheading:

Sentry Sound gives artists, producers, studios, labels, publishers, managers, and rights teams one workspace for songs, contributors, splits, files, evidence, workflow status, and operational next steps.

Recommended hero proof points:

- Know what is complete, missing, or blocked
- Organize songs, contributors, files, and evidence
- Prepare for more structured rights and submission workflows

## 4. CTA Wording

Primary CTA:

Start Free Trial

Secondary CTA:

View Plans

Header CTA:

Login

Current routing:

- Login routes to `/sign-in`.
- Start Free Trial routes to `/sign-in` until a dedicated registration/start-trial route exists.
- View Plans routes to the landing-page plans section.

## 5. Audience Positioning

The page should clearly speak to:

- artists
- producers
- studios
- labels
- publishers
- managers
- rights administrators
- operational and compliance teams

The page should avoid speaking only to a single creator persona. Sentry Sound is a music-operations platform, not a fan, streaming, or social music product.

## 6. Practical Problems To Name

Use plain-language industry pain points:

- Ownership details are unclear.
- Contributor data is incomplete.
- Splits are hard to validate.
- Files and agreements are scattered.
- Evidence is missing or hard to find.
- Teams do not know what is ready or blocked.
- Admin work is spread across too many tools.
- Submission preparation is delayed by missing information.
- Operational history is hard to trace.

## 7. Operational Intelligence Positioning

Sentry Sound should be described as helping teams:

- track readiness
- surface missing information
- highlight blockers
- organize contributors, files, evidence, and workflow status
- improve operational visibility
- prepare for structured submission and royalty workflows later
- see next steps clearly

Avoid internal terms such as:

- operational graph
- readiness aggregation
- entitlement registry
- orchestration engine
- workflow mutation
- lifecycle abstraction

Use plain terms such as:

- ready
- missing
- blocked
- contributors
- evidence
- submissions
- workflow
- activity
- operational clarity
- organization
- tracking
- visibility
- next steps

## 9. Future-Facing Messaging Boundaries

Safe to say now:

- Sentry Sound is designed as an operational workspace for music-rights and music-operations work.
- The platform helps organize songs, contributors, splits, files, evidence, and workflow status.
- The system is being built to surface what is complete, missing, blocked, and ready for review.
- The platform prepares teams for more structured registration, submission, royalty, finance, and enterprise workflows over time.

Must be framed as future or later:

- automatic regulator submission
- live SAMRO or CAPASSO integration
- automatic royalty collection
- payout processing
- production AI recommendations
- production enterprise workflow automation
- real billing/payment integration
- production MFA and invite-email enforcement

Do not claim:

- automatic submission to industry bodies
- guaranteed royalty recovery
- legal verification of ownership
- fully production-ready evidence governance
- fully production-ready dashboard metrics
- fully active enterprise automation

## 10. Tone Guidance

The tone should be:

- modern
- practical
- calm
- confident
- commercially useful
- organized
- enterprise-ready
- approachable
- workflow-oriented

Avoid:

- hype
- fear-based language
- exaggerated AI claims
- music-streaming styling
- flashy creator-culture wording
- backend or architecture terminology

## 11. Visual Direction

The visual direction should follow the supplied canonical light-theme reference:

- light UI first
- clean white and soft neutral surfaces
- blue/purple primary action color
- semantic green, amber, red, and blue for status
- Inter-style typography
- subtle borders and shadows
- compact operational cards
- readable dashboard-like previews
- restrained gradients
- lucide-style icon direction

Dark mode is secondary and should not drive the landing-page first impression.

## 12. Deferred System Dependencies

The landing page must not block on:

- Clerk email activation
- MFA enforcement
- real billing/payment provider
- production entitlement enforcement
- production RBAC enforcement
- invite email delivery
- production dashboard metrics
- production onboarding gates

These are activation and integration dependencies for later phases.
