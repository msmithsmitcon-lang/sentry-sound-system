# Module 01 — Music Industry Fundamentals
# Competency-State & Telemetry Thinking V1

## Purpose

This document defines early competency-state and telemetry thinking for Module 01.

This is not runtime implementation yet.

It prepares the module for future AI-mediated execution, learner-state tracking, remediation routing, and competency progression.

---

# Core Principle

The AI presenter should not merely deliver content.

The AI should eventually observe learner signals, interpret competency state, guide progression, and trigger remediation where needed.

---

# Competency State Model

Each SLB should eventually support learner states such as:

- not_started
- introduced
- attempted
- partially_understood
- demonstrated
- remediating
- reassessed
- mastered

---

# Confidence Indicators

Future competency confidence may be based on:

- answer accuracy
- terminology accuracy
- workflow sequencing accuracy
- role identification accuracy
- rights-awareness accuracy
- metadata-awareness accuracy
- explanation quality
- hesitation / repeated failure patterns
- remediation success
- evidence quality where applicable

---

# Telemetry Categories

## 1. Terminology Telemetry
Tracks whether the learner understands foundational industry language.

Examples:
- role names
- rights terms
- release terms
- metadata terms
- royalty terms

---

## 2. Workflow Telemetry
Tracks whether the learner understands correct process flow.

Examples:
- song creation order
- recording workflow order
- release lifecycle order
- distribution awareness
- royalty-flow awareness

---

## 3. Role & Entity Telemetry
Tracks whether the learner understands who does what.

Examples:
- artist
- producer
- publisher
- label
- distributor
- DSP
- PRO/CMO

---

## 4. Rights Awareness Telemetry
Tracks foundational understanding of ownership and rights.

Examples:
- composition rights
- master ownership
- publishing awareness
- royalty relevance

---

## 5. Digital Industry Telemetry
Tracks digital ecosystem awareness.

Examples:
- streaming platforms
- distribution routes
- metadata importance
- platform economy

---

# Remediation Trigger Examples

Remediation may be triggered when the learner:

- confuses composition and master ownership
- cannot identify major role players
- cannot sequence a release lifecycle
- misunderstands metadata importance
- confuses distributor and DSP roles
- cannot explain basic royalty flow
- repeatedly uses incorrect terminology

---

# AI Mediation Implications

The AI should eventually be able to:

- slow down explanation
- simplify terminology
- provide examples
- ask diagnostic questions
- route learner to smaller SLBs
- re-test weak competencies
- adapt explanation style
- escalate only after competency confidence improves

# Evidence Thinking

At foundation level, evidence may be lightweight.

Examples:
- ecosystem map
- role matching exercise
- workflow sequencing exercise
- basic rights comparison
- terminology validation
- short explanation response

Evidence should support future verification but remain appropriate to foundation level.
