## 2026-05-25 - Contextual Sufficiency As Weaker-Than-Readiness Review Context

Date: 2026-05-25

Source module/feature: OOC V0 design pass

Source files:

- `docs/governance/OOC-V0-DESIGN-PASS.md`
- `docs/governance/OOC-V0-INTELLIGENCE-SWEEP.md`
- `docs/platform/OPERATIONAL-UX-GOVERNANCE.md`
- `docs/platform/INDEPENDENT-MODULE-INTEGRITY.md`

Discovery summary:

Contextual sufficiency names a bounded design concept: enough visible context for a human to understand and review the next step for an intended outcome, without claiming readiness, approval, certification, legal sufficiency, or execution eligibility.

Why it may be reusable:

Many operational systems need a pre-readiness visibility layer where records, evidence, actions, and missing context are visible before orchestration or approval can be considered.

What remains deployment-specific:

The actual context needed for Sentry Sound music operations remains local: works, contributors, splits, supporting materials, evidence, actions, commitments, and submission/readiness semantics.

Related Plexicon doctrine/primitives:

- readiness is outcome-relative
- derived state
- review before approval
- evidence supports state but does not own truth
- semantic preservation

Operational evidence/testing basis:

Design-pass concept only. No runtime implementation or testing exists yet.

Risks of universalizing:

Could become a vague readiness substitute, score, gate, or hidden approval proxy if not kept weaker than readiness and explicitly human-review oriented.

Suggested classification:

- emerging primitive

Discovery summary:

What remains deployment-specific:

Sentry Sound work basics, Song Profile fields, contributor/split semantics, supporting-material references, and music readiness language remain local runtime/domain truth.

Related Plexicon doctrine/primitives:

- derived state
- contextual sufficiency
- review before approval
- semantic preservation
- orchestration coordinates, not owns

Operational evidence/testing basis:

Observed in the Work/Song detail page and the operational completeness read model. Review/doctrine only; no runtime realignment has been implemented yet.

Risks of universalizing:

Could become over-prescriptive UX doctrine, hidden workflow gating, or a claim that backend validation automatically owns user flow.

Suggested classification:

- candidate reusable

## 2026-05-26 - Context Gap To Resolution Path

Date: 2026-05-26

Source module/feature: Work/Song Detail Operational Flow Review

Source files:

- `docs/governance/OPERATIONAL-FLOW-REVIEW-PASS.md`
- `app/dashboard/works/details/[workId]/page.tsx`
- `src/lib/work-readiness/get-work-completeness.ts`

Discovery summary:

Every detected context gap should point toward a resolving section, action, module, or "workflow coming later" explanation. A warning or missing-context item should not leave the user to infer where resolution lives.

Why it may be reusable:

The pattern can apply across readiness, evidence, finance, contributor, campaign, learning, and asset workflows where systems detect gaps but users need calm guidance rather than raw warnings.

What remains deployment-specific:

The actual resolution paths for themes, language, split sheets, contributor confirmation, and supporting materials are Sentry Sound-specific.

Related Plexicon doctrine/primitives:

- contextual sufficiency
- intervention proportionality
- operational continuity
- evidence supports state but does not own truth

Operational evidence/testing basis:

Observed mismatch between completeness outputs and current Work Detail section ordering. Review/doctrine only; action linking has not been implemented.

Risks of universalizing:

Could become forced workflow gating or coercive SaaS behavior if every gap is treated as mandatory completion.

Suggested classification:

- emerging primitive

## 2026-05-26 - Operational Mode Separation

Date: 2026-05-26

Source module/feature: Work/Song Detail Operational Flow Review

Source files:

- `docs/governance/OPERATIONAL-FLOW-REVIEW-PASS.md`
- `app/dashboard/works/details/[workId]/page.tsx`

Discovery summary:

Operational pages should distinguish capture, context, review, evidence, action, and future-intelligence modes. Mixing these modes in one unstructured scroll increases cognitive load and weakens the user's ability to understand what can be acted on now.

Why it may be reusable:

The mode separation pattern may help command-center, readiness, evidence, finance, and campaign surfaces remain calm and understandable without becoming automation or approval engines.

What remains deployment-specific:

The current modes and labels are grounded in Sentry Sound's music work/detail page and may need adaptation for other deployments.

Related Plexicon doctrine/primitives:

- independent module integrity
- review before approval
- semantic preservation
- human-guided progression

Operational evidence/testing basis:

Observed on the current Work/Song detail page after OOC V0, completeness, supporting materials, contributors, creative details, and future intelligence were placed in one flow.

Risks of universalizing:

Could become rigid design taxonomy or over-abstracted UX doctrine before multiple systems validate it.

Suggested classification:

- candidate reusable
