# Sentry Sound Academy Competency Framework V1

## Purpose

This document defines the competency progression structure for the academy.

This is NOT yet:
- NQF mapping
- credit allocation
- formal accreditation
- lesson design

This defines the operational competency ladder that future certification and runtime execution will use.

---

# Core Principle

The academy is competency-based and execution-oriented.

Competency progression must reflect:
- real operational capability
- industry execution readiness
- evidence-based competence
- workflow understanding
- governed execution ability

---

# Competency Levels

## Level 1 — Foundation

### Purpose
Learner understands the terminology, ecosystem, role players, and basic concepts.

### Characteristics
- awareness
- vocabulary
- conceptual understanding
- guided participation

### Example
- Understand what an ISRC is
- Understand royalty types
- Understand what metadata means

### Evidence Type
- quizzes
- guided exercises
- terminology validation
- basic workflow identification

---

## Level 2 — Operational

### Purpose
Learner can execute standard industry workflows with supervision.

### Characteristics
- operational execution
- process understanding
- basic compliance handling
- controlled workflow participation

### Example
- Capture metadata correctly
- Prepare registration packs
- Perform royalty tracking tasks
- Use Sentry Sound operational workflows

### Evidence Type
- practical exercises
- workflow execution
- structured submissions
- guided platform usage

---

## Level 3 — Professional

### Purpose
Learner can independently execute workflows and resolve normal operational issues.

### Characteristics
- independent execution
- decision-making
- troubleshooting
- professional operational readiness

### Example
- Resolve metadata conflicts
- Handle registration corrections
- Manage release workflows
- Validate compliance requirements

### Evidence Type
- case studies
- operational simulations
- independent workflow completion
- compliance validation

---

## Level 4 — Specialist

### Purpose
Learner can manage complex operational environments and advanced industry scenarios.

### Characteristics
- advanced workflow handling
- multi-party coordination
- dispute handling
- cross-system operational capability

### Example
- International rights conflicts
- Complex royalty reconciliations
- Multi-territory licensing
- Governance workflow management

### Evidence Type
- advanced simulations
- forensic reviews
- workflow orchestration
- complex scenario handling

---

## Level 5 — Governance / Expert

### Purpose
Learner can oversee systems, governance, auditability, operational controls, and institutional execution.

### Characteristics
- governance
- audit oversight
- enterprise administration
- policy interpretation
- operational authority

### Example
- Audit workflow oversight
- Enterprise compliance governance
- Multi-workspace operational administration
- Institutional reporting and controls

### Evidence Type
- governance reviews
- audit reports
- enterprise operational simulations
- policy enforcement scenarios

---

# Important Architecture Rule

The same module may exist at multiple competency levels.

Example:

Module:
Registrations & Compliance

Foundation:
Understand registration bodies

Operational:
Submit compliant registration packs

Professional:
Resolve registration issues

Specialist:
Handle international registration disputes

Governance:
Oversee institutional compliance systems
